Hi! Welcome to my personal website. I am Ruiming Min (/ʐweɪ mɪŋ mɪn/, or "Ray Ming Min" for pronunciation, 闵锐明 in Chinese). You can also call me Raymon.

I am a Ph.D. student in Statistics at the [School of Statistics, University of Minnesota](http://www.stat.umn.edu/), working under the supervision of Professor [Xiaotong Shen](http://users.stat.umn.edu/~xshen/). Before coming to Minnesota, I completed my undergraduate studies in [Mathematics and Economics](https://maec.hkust.edu.hk/home), with a second major in [Computer Science](https://cse.hkust.edu.hk/bsc/), at [the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology](https://hkust.edu.hk/).

My research interests include generative inference, prediction and inference for black-box learners, causal relations, privacy-aware statistical learning, and synthetic data generated by modern generative models. I am always happy to connect with people working on related problems.

During my undergraduate studies, I collaborated with Professor [Xinzhou GUO](https://www.math.hkust.edu.hk/people/faculty/profile/xinzhoug/) on differential privacy in high-dimensional linear models, and with Professor [Junhui WANG](https://sites.google.com/site/junhuiwang/Home?authuser=0) on privacy-aware synthetic data generation and causal relations.
